## Lesson Plan

### 1. Entry Ticket & Hook (5 min)

**Hook Question:**  
Why do astronauts float inside the ISS when gravity is still 90% as strong as on Earth?

**Activity:**  
Students complete a 3-question entry quiz individually. Teacher presents the hook question and facilitates a brief discussion using interactive polling.

**MythBuster:**  
Heavier objects do NOT fall faster — without air resistance, all objects accelerate at 9.8 m/s² regardless of mass.

**Differentiation (IEP):** Visual concept map provided. Digital entry ticket with adjustable font size.

---

### 2. Productive Struggle (20 min)

**Activity:**  
Students work in heterogeneous groups of 3-4 on a Level 2 Kinematics worksheet. Each group receives a different intentional error to identify and correct. Teacher circulates providing scaffolding prompts.

**Group Rule:**  
Each member must contribute at least one idea using "talking chips". Surrender a chip each time you speak. When chips are exhausted, it is another member's turn.

**Differentiation:**
- **Support:** Prompt cards with step-by-step solution frameworks.
- **Challenge:** Advanced students develop alternative solutions without frameworks.

**Peer Grading:** After 15 minutes, exchange answers between groups. Score 1-4 using the rubric.

---

### 3. CER Challenge (10 min)

**Phenomenon:**  
Why do astronauts float in the ISS despite gravity being 90% of Earth's surface gravity?

**CER Template:**
- **CLAIM:** Answer the phenomenon in one sentence.
- **EVIDENCE:** Data, calculations, or observations supporting the claim.
- **REASONING:** Scientific explanation connecting evidence to claim.

**Activity:** Students independently write Claim, Evidence, and Reasoning. Two volunteers present.

---

### 4. Wrap-up & Mistake Journal (5 min)

**Reflection Prompts:**
1. Which concept was most confusing today?
2. What errors did you make while solving problems?
3. On a scale of 1-5, how well do you understand this topic?
4. What question do you still have for the next session?

**Activity:** Students write in their digital Mistake Journal. Teacher previews next week's topic.

## Worksheet — Level 2: Mistake Hunter (15 min)

A car travels 100 m north in 5 s, then 60 m south in 3 s.  
The solution contains an intentional error — find and correct it.

**Solution Steps:**
- Total distance = 160 m, total time = 8 s → speed = 20 m/s
- Displacement = 40 m north → velocity = 5 m/s north

---

## Worksheet — Level 3: CER Challenge (10 min)

A ball is dropped from 2 m and only bounces to 1.2 m.  
Write a CER argument explaining where the energy goes.

**Model Answer:**
- **CLAIM:** Energy is not lost — it transforms into thermal and sound energy.
- **EVIDENCE:** EP_initial = mg(2), EP_final = mg(1.2), difference = 0.8mg.
- **REASONING:** Conservation of energy states energy cannot be created or destroyed.
